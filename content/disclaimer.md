When we use phrases like "Ask MLK" or "What would Hamilton say?", that language is shorthand for a source-grounded AI reconstruction based on available writings, speeches, interviews, letters, books, and other approved materials.

## AI Personas Are Interpretive Reconstructions

The figures on Legends Library are not the real people. They are AI-generated interpretations built from source libraries.

The system may produce:

- Direct historical answers when the source record clearly addresses a topic
- Modern inferences when applying a figure's documented ideas to a new issue
- Lower-confidence answers when the available evidence is limited
- Refusals or uncertainty notes when the source record is too weak

No answer should be treated as proof that the actual person would have said, believed, endorsed, or supported the output.

## Sources, Citations, and Limits

Legends Library is designed to ground answers in primary sources whenever possible, including speeches, letters, books, essays, interviews, transcripts, and verified writings.

Citations are provided to help users evaluate the answer. However, citations do not guarantee that every inference is correct. Historical interpretation can be contested, incomplete, or uncertain.

Users should treat answers as educational and interpretive content — not as definitive historical authority.

## Confidence Labels

Answers may include a confidence label: **Strong**, **Inferred**, or **Refused**. These labels reflect how strongly the available source material supports the answer; they are not guarantees of truth.

- **Strong** generally means the figure addressed the topic, or a closely related principle, directly.
- **Inferred** generally means the answer applies the figure's documented ideas to a modern issue the figure did not directly address.
- **Refused** means the source record was too thin to answer honestly, so the figure declined.
