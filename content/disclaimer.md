# Disclaimer

*Last updated: June 12, 2026*

## AI Personas Trained on Primary Sources — Not Impersonations

The figures on Legends Library are **not impersonations of real people**. They are AI language models trained on each figure's documented writings — their letters, speeches, books, interviews, sermons, testimony, and other primary sources — for the specific purpose of interpreting how that figure's documented worldview and reasoning might apply to questions being asked today.

When you ask a question, you are not communicating with the real person, their estate, a representative, or anyone authorized to speak for them. You are engaging with an AI that has been trained to reason in the manner of, and from the documented perspective of, that person. Every response is a scholarly interpretation constructed by machine learning, not a statement of the real individual.

Nothing generated by Legends Library should be presented, quoted, or shared as a genuine new statement of any real person. Our AI personas will never claim to be the actual individual they represent, and responses that could be mistaken for authentic statements should be shared with appropriate context identifying them as AI-generated interpretations.

## How Responses Are Labeled — Including Topics Beyond the Documented Record

**Every response on Legends Library carries a confidence label.** Our figures will engage with any question a user asks — including questions about modern events that postdate a figure's life — because the intellectual and moral frameworks these individuals developed are often precisely what makes them relevant to contemporary debates. The confidence label tells you how grounded the response is:

- **High confidence** — The response rests on direct documentary evidence: a quote, a documented position, an explicit stance the figure took on this or a closely analogous question.
- **Medium confidence** — The evidence points in a clear direction but is not explicit. The figure's documented worldview, values, and reasoning are applied to a question they did not directly address.
- **Low confidence / Interpretation** — The question falls substantially outside the documented record. The figure's AI persona will engage, but will clearly indicate that this is an interpretive extrapolation from their broader worldview, not a position the real person ever took. These responses are explicitly labeled as interpretations and should be understood as reasoned speculation, not historical record.

We do not program our figures to refuse questions because of the year they were born or died. We believe it is more intellectually honest — and more useful — to give you a transparent, labeled interpretation than to decline entirely. A "Low confidence — interpretation" response that explains its own uncertainty is more valuable than a refusal.

## How We Source and License Our Training Content

Legends Library takes the legal acquisition of source material seriously. Our sourcing methodology is designed to respect the rights of authors, publishers, and estates while operating within established legal frameworks for educational and transformative use.

**Physical acquisition:** Where source materials are under copyright and not otherwise available in the public domain, our standard practice is to purchase physical copies of books and printed materials through normal commercial channels — the same channels available to any member of the public. These legally purchased copies are then digitized in-house using optical character recognition (OCR) technology to extract the text for use in training and retrieval.

**Legal basis:** The right to purchase a work and use it for private study, analysis, and research is a foundational principle of copyright law. The transformative use doctrine under 17 U.S.C. § 107 has been recognized by federal courts, including in landmark decisions involving the large-scale digitization of copyrighted works, as permitting uses that are educational, analytical, and non-substitutive — meaning uses that do not replace the market for the original work. In *Authors Guild v. Google* (2d Cir. 2015), the court held that digitizing and analyzing texts to extract meaning and enable new forms of engagement with them constitutes transformative fair use. Federal courts in California and elsewhere have continued to develop and apply these principles to AI training contexts. Our use of legally purchased works for training an AI that produces summarized, cited, and attributed interpretations — not reproductions — is designed to fall squarely within this framework.

**Per-source limits:** We track how much of any single work is quoted or directly referenced in responses and enforce hard per-source limits that keep total usage well within fair-use bounds regardless of how many users interact with a given figure over time.

**Public domain priority:** Where sources are in the public domain, we use them. Copyrighted material supplements the public domain record for figures whose most important works remain under copyright.

**Rights-holder requests:** We respect rights holders. If you represent the estate or publisher of a work and believe our use exceeds fair use, contact us at legal@legendslibrary.app. We will review the use and, where appropriate, restrict or remove the material.

## Fair-Use Quoting Policy

Legends Library is built to respect the rights of authors, publishers, and estates in the direct output it delivers to users.

- Direct quotations from copyrighted sources in responses are limited to **brief excerpts of no more than 300 characters**, used strictly for commentary, scholarship, and education — not reproduction.
- Each figure operates under a **per-source quote budget** that caps how much of any single work can be quoted across all responses, keeping cumulative usage well within fair-use limits.
- Quotations are always attributed to their source. Where a source cannot be quoted within these limits, the system paraphrases and cites instead.

## Right of Publicity — No Likeness Reproduction

Legends Library is designed with the Right of Publicity in mind. The Right of Publicity protects individuals — and in many jurisdictions, their estates — from the unauthorized commercial use of their name, voice, image, or likeness.

We have structured our product specifically to respect these rights:

- **No synthetic voice.** Legends Library does not generate, synthesize, or reproduce the voice of any real person, living or dead. All content is text-based.
- **No video or audiovisual likeness.** We do not create deepfake video, AI-generated audio, or any audiovisual simulation of any person. Our figures exist solely in text.
- **Figures do not claim to be the real person.** Our AI personas are instructed never to assert that they are the actual individual they represent. They speak from the perspective of a figure's documented worldview; they do not impersonate.
- **Portraits are illustrative artwork**, not photographs or likeness-based depictions implying endorsement or authenticity.
- **Debate and council features** are clearly labeled as simulated, text-based exercises in perspective, not authentic representations of what these individuals said to one another.

Our text-only, clearly-labeled, non-impersonation format is designed to avoid Right of Publicity claims while still enabling the legitimate educational purpose of engaging with the documented ideas of significant historical and public figures.
